Familial hypercholesterolemia is a medical condition characterized by elevated serum cholesterol caused by genetic defects that can be passed to the patients offsprings. The main genes that can cause the condition are LDL receptor (most common), apolipoprotein B (ApoB), or proprotein convertase subtilisin/Kexin type 9 (PCSK9). *Interpretation should be made by the physician who ordered the test or by the genetic counselor.
